Claire Beardsley was cleaning out the attic in an old Southern California Craftsman House and discovered a box full of papers, letters, and documents gathered by a woman who was active in the women's movement in the 1940s. Among the documents was a letter signed by Alice Paul who at the time was a pivotal person in the women's suffrage movement; she wrote the equal rights amendment. Claire Beardsley was herself active in the Women's Movement in the 60s and thus was excited to find this box of over 100 documents. In the late 60's Claire made a film of women doing what they did, grocery shopping, laundry, and if you get bored watching this movie, that is exactly the point, women had few other options.
