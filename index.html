Women's Movement In The 1940s

Home The Letter Booklets Articles About Us The Movie

Claire Beardsley was cleaning out the attic in an old Southern California Craftsman House and discovered a box full of papers, letters, and documents gathered by a woman who was active in the women's movement in the 1940s. In it was a letter by the woman, who at the time was a pivotal person in the women's suffrage movement, Alice Paul. She wrote the equal rights amendment, as well. Claire Beardsley was herself active in the Women's Movement in the 60s and thus was excited to find this box of over a thousand documents. In the late 60's Claire made a film of women doing what they did, grocery shopping, laundry, and if you get bored watching this movie, that is exactly the point, women had few other options.
