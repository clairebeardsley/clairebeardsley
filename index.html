Claire Beardsley was cleaning out the attic in an old Southern California Craftsman House and discovered a box full of papers, letters, and documents gathered by a woman who was active in the women's movement in the 1940s. Among the documents was a letter signed by Alice Paul who at the time was a pivotal person in the women's suffrage movement; she wrote the equal rights amendment.
